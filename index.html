The Problem Statement

Challenge 1: Visual Overload

Most AAC (Augmentative & Alternative Communication) apps display 20‚Äì30 icons simultaneously, causing sensory anxiety in autistic teens.

Challenge 2: Infantilizing Design

Current apps use bright colors and cartoons. 14-year-olds feel embarrassed using them in school settings.

Challenge 3: Poor Motor Accessibility

Small touch targets (< 44px) cause frequent mis-taps for users with fine motor coordination challenges.

Accessibility Needs:

Key Insights from Observation

Cognitive Overload: When presented with >12 choices, Ahmed becomes frustrated and disengages. He prefers sequential, linear task flows.

Motor Challenges: Ahmed uses his whole hand to interact with screens (not just fingers). Small targets cause frequent mis-taps and frustration.

Social Awareness: At 14, Ahmed is very aware he's using "babyish" apps. He wants something that looks mature and doesn't draw attention in school.

Need for Control: Ahmed responds well to clear feedback ("You said: water"), visual confirmation, and a visible "Home" escape route.

3. Assignment 2: App Review Synthesis

What Competitors Got Wrong (So We Got It Right)

‚ùå Avaz AAC (Top Competitor)

  • Issue: 30+ icons visible at once
  • Impact: Overwhelming for Ahmed; he couldn't focus on relevant options
  • Our Fix: Display only 3 main categories, progressive disclosure
  • Issue: Bright neon colors + animations
  • Impact: Visual sensory overload; increased anxiety
  • Our Fix: Calm, muted teal palette; no auto-animations

‚ùå JABtalk (Second Review)

  • Issue: Touch targets ~30px (too small)
  • Impact: 70% mis-tap rate for Ahmed; very frustrating
  • Our Fix: Minimum 60px touch targets; large, forgiving buttons
  • Issue: Kiddie graphics (balloons, cartoons)
  • Impact: Embarrassing in school; Ahmed refused to use it
  • Our Fix: Modern, neutral design that doesn't signal "kids app"

üéØ Design Principles We Adopted

1. Progressive Disclosure: Start with 3 choices, reveal more only when needed.

2. Sensory Safety: Muted palette, no flash animations, calm visual hierarchy.

3. Large Touch Targets: All buttons ‚â•48px to accommodate motor challenges.

4. Consistent Patterns: Predictable flows reduce cognitive load; every screen has "Home" button.
